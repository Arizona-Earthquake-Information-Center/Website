June, 16, 2011

Between Tusayan and Red Butte, AZ

 

Nine earthquakes occured last week between Tusayan, Arizona and Red Butte, Arizona. The majority of events occured on Thursday, June 16th and were between duration magnitude 1.7 and 2.8. The swarm occurred over a period of five days  at a depth of between 3.5-10 km deep. The nearest fault system is the northeast-southwest trending Bright Angel fault zone which extends through the Grand Canyon. In general, this event cluster lies south of the dominant Grand Canyon area seismicity.
