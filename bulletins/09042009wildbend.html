September 4, 2009

Wild Bend Earthquake

 

A magnitude 3.5 earthquake struck about 25 miles south of Colorado City, Arizona at 4:48 a.m. local time.  The earthquake was felt by residents of Pipe Springs National Monument.  At 4:53 a.m. a magnitude 2.8 aftershock occurred about 2 miles north of the main shock.  The earthquakes are located in the area of the Toroweap fault zone and a preliminary focal mechanism suggests normal faulting on a northeast trending fault.   

 

The earthquake data are as follows:

  • Magnitude (ML) 3.5 Friday, September 4, 2009 11:47:53 UTC 36.62N x 112.87W
  • Magnitude (ML) 2.8 Friday, September 4, 2009 11:53:46 UTC 36.66N x 112.88W
